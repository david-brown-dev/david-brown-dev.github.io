Emotional Intelligence

What is it and why is it important?

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is the ability to identify and deal with your own emotions as well as the emotions of others around you. There are generally thought to be 3 key skills associated with emotional intelligence:

  • The ability to recognize your own emotions
  • The ability to use those emotions in tasks like thinking or problem solving
  • The ability to manage your emotions when necessary for example when you need to cheer someone else up

IQ or Intelligence Quotient is a measure of your reasoning ability and covers how well you can use information and logic to answer questions or make predictions. It covers areas like memorisation, spatial ability and mathematical comprehension etc. EQ on the other hand is as mentioned above, the ability to recognise and manage one’s own emotions and the emotions of others.

Why is it important?

Workplaces are environments full of relationships. They are a melting pot of different personalities, skills, strengths, and emotions. All of this is trying to fulfil the purpose of the business.

So EQ is involved in each and every decision and action within the workplace. It’s there when pair programming just as much as coordinating sweeping organisational changes. Therefore people with a high EQ will typically be better placed to better navigate the workplace and all of the intricacies of relationships that make it up.

In contrast, a low EQ can be extremely detrimental in the workplace, leading to aggression, bullying, arrogance and other demotivating workplace behaviours.
